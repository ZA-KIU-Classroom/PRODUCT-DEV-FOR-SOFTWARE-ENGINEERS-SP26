## Overview

Lab 12 feeds directly into Checkpoint 4, which is worth 15 points and assessed in two parts: the live pitch and Q&A at Demo Day (10 points), and the repository review conducted asynchronously by judges after the event (5 points).

In-lab participation is assessed against the participation component of the course grade (5 points total across all weeks).

---

## Part A: Live Pitch and Q&A at Demo Day (10 points)

Assessed by external judges at Demo Day on Thursday 11 June 2026.

### A1: Problem Clarity (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Problem is stated in one sentence. ICP is named and specific. Pain intensity is quantified or evidenced with a real quote. Judges can immediately identify who suffers and why it matters. |
| 1 | Problem is described but either the ICP is vague, the pain intensity is asserted without evidence, or it takes more than one slide worth of explanation. |
| 0 | Problem is generic, jargon-heavy, or takes multiple minutes to establish. Judges cannot name the person who experiences the pain after the slide passes. |

### A2: Product Demonstrated Live (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Live deployed product shown on a real device during the pitch. Core user flow completed end-to-end. Product works without error in the room. URL is stated. |
| 1 | Product is shown but as a screen recording, Figma prototype, or screenshot sequence rather than a live interaction. Or product partially fails during demonstration. |
| 0 | No product demonstrated. Or demonstration fails completely and team cannot recover. |

### A3: Traction and Business Model (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Real user or usage numbers stated from analytics. Retention or engagement metric included. CAC and LTV stated with their source explained. Numbers are internally consistent across all slides. |
| 1 | Numbers presented but unverified, inconsistent between slides, or stated without explanation of how they were obtained. Business model described but unit economics incomplete. |
| 0 | No real numbers. Projections only. Business model is vague or absent. "We will figure out monetisation later" is a zero. |

### A4: Competitive Moat (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Competitive matrix shown with 5 or more named competitors and 7 or more evaluation dimensions. Moat named explicitly using one of Helmer's Seven Powers. At least two pieces of evidence from the repository cited. "There is no real competition" is not said. |
| 1 | Competitive analysis present but fewer than 5 competitors named, or the moat is asserted without evidence, or the named power cannot be evidenced. |
| 0 | No competitive analysis. Or "there is no real competition" stated. Or generic competitive slide that could apply to any product. |

### A5: Q&A Fluency (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Team answers directly and concisely. Numbers are owned by the team without hesitation. Objections acknowledged honestly. Team acknowledges what they do not know rather than deflecting. |
| 1 | Answers are evasive, circular, or rely on restating slide content. One or more numbers in the deck cannot be explained by the team when asked. |
| 0 | Team cannot answer basic questions about their own product. Numbers in the deck contradict each other when probed. Team becomes defensive or dismissive under questioning. |

## Part B: Repository Review (5 points)

Assessed asynchronously by judges after Demo Day. Repository must be public by Demo Day start time.

### B1: Folder Completeness (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | All folders from `00-foundation` through `09-final` present and non-empty. Key files exist in each folder as specified in the Checkpoint 4 requirements. |
| 1 | Most folders present but 1 to 3 required folders are missing or empty. |
| 0 | Fewer than half the required folders are present, or repository is effectively empty beyond source code. |

### B2: README and Documentation Quality (2 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | README contains product name, tagline, problem statement, live URL that works, demo video link, team members, tech stack, setup instructions, and open-source licence declaration. All links resolve. |
| 1 | README exists but missing 2 to 4 required elements, or live URL is broken, or demo video link is missing. |
| 0 | README is the default GitHub template, does not mention the product, or the repository is missing a README entirely. |

### B3: Investor Materials (1 point)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | `05-fundraising/pitch-deck.pdf` and `05-fundraising/one-pager.pdf` both present as valid PDF files with real content. |
| 0.5 | One of the two files is present or both exist but one is a placeholder with no real content. |
| 0 | Neither file is present. |

## In-Lab Participation (Observed by Instructor)

Instructor circulates and checks progress at 0:15, 0:45, 1:15, and 1:50.

| Checkpoint | What Is Checked | Full Credit | Partial Credit | No Credit |
|------------|----------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| 0:15 | Repository audit complete | Gap list documented with owners assigned | Audit started but incomplete | Not attempted |
| 0:45 | Pitch deck in progress | At least 6 of 10 slides drafted with real content | Fewer than 6 slides or slides contain only headers | Not started |
| 1:15 | One-pager drafted | All 8 sections filled with real content | Fewer than 5 sections filled | Not started |
| 1:50 | Committed and pushed | `git push` confirmed and files visible in public repo | Files created but not committed | Nothing committed |

Participation is scored as part of the weekly participation grade, not as part of Checkpoint 4. However, a team that does not commit by 1:50 will need to resolve this before Demo Day.

---

## Grade Deductions

The following issues result in automatic deductions from the Checkpoint 4 repository review score regardless of other quality:

| Issue | Deduction |
|-------|-----------|
| Repository is private at time of judge review | Minus 2 points |
| Live product URL returns 404 or error | Minus 1 point |
| `pitch-deck.pdf` missing from `05-fundraising/` | Minus 1 point |
| No open-source licence file in repository root | Minus 0.5 points |
| README has no live product URL | Minus 0.5 points |

---

## What Checkpoint 4 Is Worth in the Final Grade

Checkpoint 4 is worth 15 points total in the course grade:

- Live pitch and Q&A (Demo Day): 10 points
- Repository review: 5 points

Individual grade adjustment from the peer assessment (completed in the Week 14 lecture) applies to all team checkpoint scores, including Checkpoint 4, up to plus or minus 20 percent.
