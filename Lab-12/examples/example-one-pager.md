## Design notes for your own one-pager

The example above contains 271 words across all sections. This is within the 300-word total limit and fits comfortably on a single A4 page at 11pt body text.

**What this example does right:**

The live URL appears three times: in the header, beneath the product name, and in the contact section. A judge reading this at any point on the page can immediately find the product. This is intentional.

The traction section uses a table rather than prose. Three numbers in a table are faster to read and harder to misinterpret than three numbers embedded in a sentence.

The team section gives one concrete credential per person rather than aspiration. "Full-stack developer, 2 years React and Node.js, deployed 3 production applications" is a credential. "Passionate about technology and building products that matter" is not.

The business model section shows the LTV:CAC ratio explicitly. Many student teams include CAC and LTV separately but do not compute the ratio. Judges will compute it themselves if you do not, and a ratio that is not stated signals you have not thought about what it means.

**What this example omits:**

There is no "Ask" section in this example. For Demo Day purposes, the ask is optional on the one-pager because the pitch deck covers it. If you are genuinely pursuing investment, add the ask as an eighth section at the bottom.

There are no team photos in the text representation. In the actual designed PDF, a small professional photo (2cm by 2cm headshot) sits beside each team member's name. If your team has professional photos available, include them. If not, a clean typographic layout without photos is acceptable.
