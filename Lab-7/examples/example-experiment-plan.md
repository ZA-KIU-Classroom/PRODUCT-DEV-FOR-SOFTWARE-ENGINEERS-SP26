# Example Experiment Plan

**Team:** StudySpace Finders  
**Product:** StudySpace  
**Date launched:** 23 April 2026  
**Owner:** Nino

> Fictional example for structure only.

---

## 1. Hypothesis

We believe KIU students who lose study time to space hunting will sign up for occupancy alerts at 20% or more because they want a reliable signal before leaving home.

---

## 2. Assumption Being Tested

Students care enough about unreliable study space availability to exchange an email address for alert access before a full product exists.

---

## 3. Top 3 Riskiest Assumptions

| Rank | Assumption | Why risky | Why this experiment addresses it |
|------|------------|----------|----------------------------------|
| 1 | Students want alert access badly enough to sign up now | The team may be solving a mild annoyance, not a painful problem | Signup behavior tests demand directly |
| 2 | The occupancy alert message is understandable in one screen | Messaging may be vague or too abstract | Landing page forces a clear value proposition |
| 3 | Students will respond through digital channels rather than in person persuasion | Channel may be weak | Distribution through course groups and student communities tests reach |

---

## 4. Experiment Method

**Method chosen:** Smoke test

- Channel: student group chats, university Discord, and targeted outreach to student communities
- Asset used: Carrd landing page with one headline, one visual, one short explanation, and one signup form
- Call to action: Join the early access list for occupancy alerts
- Real target users are reached by: communities where students already discuss study routines
- What happens after a user responds: email captured, short follow up question asks how they currently find space

---

## 5. Success, Gray Zone, Failure

- Success threshold: 20% or more visitor to signup conversion
- Gray zone: 10% to 19%
- Failure threshold: below 10%

---

## 6. Time Window and Sample Size

- Experiment starts: 23 April 2026
- Experiment ends: 5 May 2026
- Minimum sample target: 100 unique visitors
- What counts as one valid data point: one unique visit from a real target user

---

## 7. Data Capture Plan

| Signal | How captured | Where recorded | Owner |
|--------|--------------|---------------|------|
| Visits | Carrd analytics | experiment tracking sheet | Nino |
| Clicks or form submits | Form analytics | experiment tracking sheet | Nino |
| Signups | email capture form | waitlist spreadsheet | Ana |
| Follow up notes | short user replies | interview extension notes | David |

---

## 8. Live Asset Checklist

- [x] Landing page or form is published or scheduled
- [x] Channel for real users is selected
- [x] Success and failure thresholds are frozen
- [x] Team knows who monitors the experiment
- [x] Decision review date is set

---

## 9. Decision Rule

- If result meets success threshold, we will continue Sprint 1 with confidence and tighten the booking flow around alert demand
- If result falls below failure threshold, we will revisit the core problem statement and test a narrower pain such as space search only

---

## 10. What Would Make This Experiment Invalid

- Most traffic comes from teammates or friends already invested in the project
- Page copy changes halfway through the experiment without logging the change
- Visitor count is tiny and below the minimum sample
