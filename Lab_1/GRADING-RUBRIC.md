# Lab 1 Grading Rubric

**Course:** CS-PD-2026  
**Total Points:** 15  
**Deadline:** Thursday, March 19, 2026 at 11:59 PM  

Lab 1 contributes to the Participation and Labwork component (10% of final grade, assessed weekly across the semester).

---

## Component 1: Team Contract (4 points)

File: `00-foundation/team-contract.md`

**4 points -- Excellent**  
All required sections are complete. Roles are clearly assigned with named individuals. The decision-making protocol specifies the tie-breaking mechanism. The conflict protocol follows the KIU 4-step structure (verbal debate, written view, cool-off, Program Lead decision). Communication cadence is specific (e.g., "standup twice weekly on Tuesday and Thursday via WhatsApp at 20:00"). Contribution tracking mechanism is documented. All team members have reviewed and the document is committed to GitHub.

**3 points -- Good**  
All sections present and mostly complete. One or two sections have placeholder text that was not fully filled in. Core role assignments and conflict protocol are present and functional.

**2 points -- Acceptable**  
Most sections present but notable gaps. Conflict protocol is absent or too vague to be actionable. Roles may be assigned but accountabilities are unclear.

**1 point -- Incomplete**  
The document exists but is largely a template with unfilled placeholders. Less than half of the required content is present.

**0 points**  
File not submitted, or the document is a copy of the template with no team-specific content.

## Component 2: Individual Problem Statements (5 points)

File: `00-foundation/all-problem-statements.md`

Assessment is on the collective quality of the document, which must show that each member genuinely engaged in individual, observation-based brainstorming.

**5 points -- Excellent**  
Every team member has submitted exactly 3 problems, one per H category. All problems are grounded in direct personal observation or lived experience -- no hypotheticals, no AI-generated content, no problems sourced from blog posts or general knowledge. At least two-thirds of all problems follow the WHO / WHAT / WHEN / WHY structure with meaningful specificity. The document shows diverse problem spaces -- not all problems are from the same domain. There is no evidence of premature convergence (all problems pushed toward a single theme before Four Filters).

**4 points -- Good**  
All members have submitted 3 problems in 3H structure. Most are grounded in observation. Specificity could be stronger on 2-3 problems but the core quality is solid.

**3 points -- Acceptable**  
All members have submitted 3 problems but 3H categorisation is unclear or some problems are clearly hypothetical. Specificity is low -- most problems are at the level of "people have trouble with X" without concrete who/when/why.

**2 points -- Weak**  
One or more team members have not submitted their individual problems. Or the document shows evidence of group writing (all problems obviously edited to match a single predetermined direction) rather than individual brainstorming.

**1 point -- Incomplete**  
Fewer than half of the required problem statements are present, or the content is clearly AI-generated with no grounding in personal observation.

**0 points**  
File not submitted.

**Automatic deduction of 1 point:** If the document contains a "team selected problem" or equivalent section, or if it is clear that the team narrowed to a single problem during Lab 1 rather than preserving the full exploration. Lab 1 ends with no team problem selected.

## Component 3: Two-Candidate ICP Profiles (3 points)

File: `00-foundation/team-icp.md`

**3 points -- Excellent**  
Two distinct candidate ICP profiles are present, each clearly tied to a named candidate problem from the `all-problem-statements.md` document. Each profile includes: the specific user segment, the context (when and where the problem occurs for this user), the pain intensity and nature, and a brief rationale for why this segment is a promising target. Profiles are sufficiently different from each other -- not two near-identical descriptions with different labels.

**2 points -- Good**  
Both profiles are present and linked to candidate problems. One profile is stronger than the other. Missing one or two of the required sub-components but the core picture is clear.

**1 point -- Incomplete**  
Only one ICP profile submitted, or both profiles are so similar that they effectively represent a single direction. Or the profiles are not linked to specific candidate problems.

**0 points**  
File not submitted or contains only a single committed ICP (which would indicate a problem was selected in Lab 1 in violation of the sequencing requirement).

## Component 4: GitHub Repository Structure (2 points)

**2 points**  
Repository exists under the correct GitHub organisation. All required folders are present (`00-foundation/`, `01-discovery/`, `milestones/`). All team members are listed as collaborators with push access confirmed. `README.md` is present with the team name and a brief description of the project. Commit history shows commits from at least two different team members by the deadline. The `lab-1-submission` tag is present.

**1 point**  
Repository exists and has the correct structure but one of the following is missing: not all team members are collaborators, `README.md` is empty, commit history shows only one contributor, or the submission tag is absent.

**0 points**  
Repository does not exist, is private and inaccessible to the instructor, or has none of the required structure.

## Important Notes for Graders

**On premature convergence:** If the `all-problem-statements.md` file shows all individual problems pointing toward a single domain or solution direction, and especially if any document states "the team has selected X as our problem," deduct 1 point from Component 2 and note this in the feedback. This is the key failure mode from the previous semester and the sequencing of this lab is specifically designed to prevent it.

**On AI-generated content:** Problem statements that read as generic, lack personal grounding, and use language that no student would naturally use ("optimising operational workflows in enterprise environments") should be flagged. Ask the student to point to the specific observation or experience that generated the problem. If they cannot, award 0 for that problem statement.

**On the ICP file:** A single committed ICP rather than two candidate ICPs is a signal that the team selected a problem in Lab 1. Treat this the same as a submitted team problem statement: note it in feedback and deduct from Component 3.
