## Part A: Team Synthesis Deliverables (Graded as part of Checkpoint 1, 10 points)

Checkpoint 1 encompasses the entire discovery phase output. The synthesis deliverables from Lab 4 are the final component of Checkpoint 1. Teams that submitted partial Checkpoint 1 materials on April 1 should finalize all synthesis documents by April 8.

### Checkpoint 1 Rubric Breakdown

| Component | Points | Criteria for Full Marks |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|
| Interview Logs (quantity and quality) | 2.0 | 10+ interview logs, each with 3+ verbatim quotes, logged within 24 hours, proper format |
| ICP Document | 1.0 | Evidence-based, refined from interview data, clear demographics and psychographics |
| Affinity Map | 1.5 | 50+ insights extracted, 5 to 8 labeled clusters, photo evidence included, outliers documented |
| Pattern Analysis | 2.0 | Frequency table complete, pain intensity rated with quotes, Five Whys for top 3 patterns, clear ranking |
| Final Problem Statement | 2.0 | All 5 components (WHO/WHAT/WHEN-WHERE/WHY/IMPACT), cites 8+ interviews, quantified impact, passes stranger test |
| Competitive Landscape Seed | 0.5 | All alternatives from interviews listed, organized table, at least one quote per entry |
| Repository Organization | 1.0 | All files in correct paths, clean commit messages, no missing documents |
| **Total** | **10.0** | |

### Grading Scale per Component

**Full marks:** Meets all criteria, evidence is thorough, writing is clear.

**75% of marks:** Meets most criteria but has gaps. For example: pattern analysis is complete but Five Whys only done for 2 patterns instead of 3, or problem statement cites 6 interviews instead of 8.

**50% of marks:** Significant gaps. For example: affinity map has only 25 notes instead of 50, or problem statement is vague and does not cite specific interviews.

**25% of marks:** Minimal effort. Document exists but is largely incomplete or does not use interview evidence.

**0 marks:** Document missing or entirely fabricated (no connection to actual interview data).

## Part B: HW1 Individual Interview Contribution Log (5 Points)

### Purpose

HW1 ensures every team member personally conducted customer discovery interviews rather than relying on one or two teammates to do all the work. This is graded individually.

### What to Submit

Each student creates a file called `hw1-interview-contribution-[your-name].md` and places it in the root of their team repository OR submits it via the designated submission method announced in class.

### Required Contents

1. **Your name and team name**
2. **List of interviews you personally conducted** (minimum 3), including:
   - Interview number (matching the team's interview log numbering)
   - Date conducted
   - Duration
   - Your role (sole interviewer, lead interviewer, or note-taker who asked follow-up questions)
   - One key insight you personally extracted from that interview
3. **Reflection paragraph** (150 to 300 words): What did you learn about interviewing? What would you do differently? What surprised you about what users said?

### HW1 Rubric

| Component | Points | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Interview Evidence | 3.0 | At least 3 interviews personally conducted, each with date, duration, role, and key insight. Interviews match the team's logs. |
| Quality of Insights | 1.0 | Key insights are specific, evidence-based, and demonstrate genuine learning from the conversation. Not generic summaries. |
| Reflection | 1.0 | Thoughtful reflection on interviewing skills, with specific examples of what worked and what did not. Shows growth. |
| **Total** | **5.0** | |

### HW1 Grading Scale

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5.0 | 3+ interviews with complete evidence, insights are sharp and specific, reflection is genuine and references specific moments |
| 4.0 | 3 interviews with evidence but some details are thin, or insights are somewhat generic, or reflection is brief |
| 3.0 | Only 2 interviews with evidence, or 3 interviews where the student's role is unclear (were they actually present?), or reflection is superficial |
| 2.0 | Only 1 interview with evidence, or interviews lack dates/durations/roles, or insights are clearly copied from team's pattern analysis |
| 1.0 | Submission exists but does not demonstrate personal interview participation |
| 0 | No submission |

### What Counts as "Personally Conducted"

- You were the lead interviewer asking questions (ideal)
- You were a co-interviewer who asked substantive follow-up questions and can demonstrate this with specific quotes you elicited
- You were the sole note-taker AND you conducted the follow-up questions

### What Does NOT Count

- You were in the room but did not speak
- You transcribed a recording someone else made
- You copied insights from a teammate's interview log
- Your interview log is identical or near-identical to a teammate's log for the same interview

### Academic Integrity

Interview logs will be cross-referenced across team members. If two students submit identical key insights for the same interview, only the lead interviewer will receive credit for that interview. If fabrication is suspected, the instructor will schedule a brief verbal check-in to confirm your interview experience.
