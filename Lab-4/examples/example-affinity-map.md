## Cluster Details

### Cluster 1: Time Wasted on Room Hunting

**Theme:** Students physically walk between buildings checking for available study rooms, often spending 20 to 40 minutes before finding a spot or giving up entirely.

**Sample insights:**

1. "I walked to three buildings last Tuesday before I found an empty room. Took me 35 minutes." (P03)
2. "Sometimes I just give up and study in my dorm, which is not ideal because my roommate is always there." (P05)
3. "I waste at least 20 minutes every time I need to study on campus. That adds up." (P02)
4. "I have a 90-minute gap between classes and I spend half of it looking for a place to sit." (P07)
5. "I once walked to the library, it was full, walked to the engineering building, also full, then just sat in the hallway." (P09)

**Initial observation:** This is the most frequently mentioned pain point and the one with the clearest quantifiable impact (time lost). The frustration is amplified because it is recurring, not a one-time event.

---

### Cluster 5: Workaround Ecosystem

**Theme:** In the absence of a reliable solution, students have created informal systems: texting friends who are already on campus, arriving 30 minutes early to "claim" spots, and using social media to check if the library looks full.

**Sample insights:**

1. "I text my friend who has class before me and ask her to save me a spot." (P04)
2. "I check the library Instagram to see if it looks crowded in the photos." (P08)
3. "I just go really early. Like 7:30 AM. That is the only way to guarantee a spot." (P02)
4. "We have a WhatsApp group where people post when they leave the library so others know a spot opened." (P10)
5. "I put my bag on a chair and then go to class. It is not great but everyone does it." (P05)

**Initial observation:** The existence of these workarounds is the strongest signal of genuine demand. People are already investing effort to solve this problem. The workarounds are social, not technological, which suggests a technology solution could be significantly better.

---

## Outliers and Surprises

1. "I actually prefer studying at a cafe off campus because the Wi-Fi is better." (P06) - This suggests the problem may not be purely about space availability but also about infrastructure quality.
2. "The university built a new study center but nobody uses it because it is too far from the main buildings." (P01) - Location matters more than capacity.
