# Example: Final Problem Statement

**Team:** StudySpace Finders  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Based on:** 10 interviews conducted between March 12 and March 28, 2026

---

## The Problem Statement

> KIU undergraduate students with 60 to 90 minute gaps between classes (approximately 400 students per semester) waste an average of 25 minutes per study attempt physically walking between buildings to find available study spaces because the university has no real-time occupancy information for informal study areas. This results in an estimated 2 to 3 lost productive hours per student per week, increased stress during midterm and exam periods, and a pattern of resignation where students default to less effective study locations (dorms, hallways, cafes) rather than continuing to search.

---

## Component Breakdown

### WHO

KIU undergraduate students in their second year or later, carrying 5 or more courses per semester, who regularly study on campus between classes rather than returning to their dorms.

**Evidence:** 8 of 10 interviewees matched this profile. "I am on campus from 9 AM to 5 PM most days. Going home between classes is not realistic." (P02)

### WHAT

Students cannot determine which study rooms, library zones, or common areas are available without physically visiting each location.

**Evidence:** "There is literally no way to know if a room is free without walking there." (P07)

### WHEN / WHERE

The problem is worst during midday hours (11 AM to 3 PM) on weekdays, and intensifies dramatically during midterm and final exam periods. It occurs across all campus buildings but is most acute in the library and engineering building.

**Evidence:** "During midterms, forget about it. Every room is full by 10 AM." (P05)

### WHY (Root Cause)

The university's room management system was designed for faculty course scheduling, not for tracking real-time walk-in occupancy. Student spontaneous study was never a design consideration.

### IMPACT

- Time lost: average 25 minutes per attempt, 2 to 3 times per week = 50 to 75 minutes per student per week
- At scale: 400 affected students x 50 minutes/week = ~333 lost student-hours per week
- Emotional cost: frustration, anxiety during exam periods, sense of wasted effort
- Academic cost: reduced study effectiveness from settling for suboptimal locations

**Evidence:** "I probably lose 2 hours a week just on this. That is a whole study session I could have had." (P02)
