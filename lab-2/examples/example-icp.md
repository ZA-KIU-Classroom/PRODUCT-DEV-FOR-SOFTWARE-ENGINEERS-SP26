# Example: Ideal Customer Profile

> This is a worked example showing what a complete, strong ICP looks like. Do not copy it. Use it to calibrate the level of specificity required.

**Team Name:** Team Spotlight (example)
**Date:** 19 March 2026
**Version:** v1.0 (pre-interview hypothesis)

---

## Primary ICP: Study Space Discovery Problem

### 1. Segment

Second-year and third-year Computer Science and Engineering students at Kutaisi International University who commute from outside the city centre by marshrutka or bus, typically arriving on campus by 9:00 and remaining until at least 17:00 due to travel time costs. They have non-consecutive timetables with at least two gaps per week of 90 minutes or longer. They are academically motivated — they intend to use these gaps for coursework — but currently cannot reliably do so.

> **Why this is specific enough:** You could stand at the bus stop outside KIU and identify three people matching this description within 20 minutes. You know their year, programme, commute situation, timetable pattern, and motivation. This is testable.

---

### 2. Context

The problem occurs during the gap periods between scheduled classes, typically between 10:00 and 16:00 on Tuesdays and Thursdays (common heavy-lecture days at KIU). The student is already on campus, has their laptop with them, and has a specific piece of coursework or study task they intend to work on. They have 90-150 minutes available and cannot justify taking the 30+ minute journey home and back.

---

### 3. Primary Pain

The student cannot determine where to go to work without physically walking through the building. The library is often at capacity for individual seats even when rooms appear unbooked. The room booking system only reflects formal reservations and not actual occupancy. The student walks from floor to floor, checking each potential space, and either finds nothing suitable or settles for a noisy environment that significantly reduces their ability to do focused work. By the time they are seated and working, 20-40 minutes of the gap has been consumed by the search.

---

### 4. Current Workaround

The student follows a three-step process. First, they message 2-4 WhatsApp groups (course group chats, flat/accommodation groups, friend groups) asking if anyone knows a free quiet spot. Response time is unpredictable and often none comes. Second, they check the university library portal, which shows room bookings but not individual seat availability, and therefore does not tell them what they actually need to know. Third, they physically walk a route through the building — typically the library lobby, the corridor outside Room 214, and the ground-floor cafeteria — before settling wherever appears least disruptive. This entire process takes 20-40 minutes and often ends in a suboptimal environment anyway.

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When I have a gap between classes, I want to immediately know where I can go on campus to do focused work, so I can use that time for coursework instead of losing it to searching and settling."

---

### 6. Success Signal

The student sits down to work within 5 minutes of the start of their gap period. They do not send messages to WhatsApp groups looking for a seat. They do not check the library portal that has proven unhelpful. They complete a meaningful unit of work during the gap — finishing a problem set, writing a section of a report — and leave campus feeling the day was productive. Observed behavioural change: the WhatsApp messages stop.

---

### 7. Where to Find Them

Specific places to recruit interviewees this week:
- The corridor outside Room 214 between 10:00 and 12:00 on Tuesdays and Thursdays — this is where the highest concentration of our ICP ends up
- The CS department student lounge during mid-morning
- The ground-floor cafeteria during peak gap hours (10:00-12:00 and 14:00-16:00)
- The KIU CS Students Facebook group (approximately 340 members)
- Direct WhatsApp to classmates we know personally match the ICP

We can identify 12 specific people by name right now. Each team member will take 3 contacts and initiate outreach by end of day today.

---

### 8. Why You, Why Now

Three of the four team members are second or third-year CS students at KIU who commute and have personally experienced this problem this week. This gives us peer-to-peer access that reduces the formality barrier in interviews. Interviewees are more likely to give honest, unguarded answers when talking to a classmate than to a stranger. We do not need to explain the context of the problem because we live it. We can conduct interviews between our own classes tomorrow without any scheduling overhead.

---

### Evidence Basis

| Dimension | Confidence | Basis |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Segment | High | Observed — three team members match exactly |
| Context | High | Observed — we know this happens on Tuesdays and Thursdays specifically |
| Primary Pain | High | Observed — we have personally walked the search route described |
| Workaround | High | Observed — we use the WhatsApp method ourselves |
| JTBD | Medium | Inferred from our own experience — needs interview validation |
| Success Signal | Low | Assumed — we do not yet know what the interviewee would notice first |

> **Note on low confidence dimensions:** Low confidence is not a problem before interviews. It means those dimensions are your priority interview questions. You are building this ICP as a hypothesis to test, not as a fact to defend.

## Secondary ICP: Code Feedback Gap Problem

### 1. Segment

First-year and second-year Computer Science students at KIU who are working on individual programming assignments and regularly encounter blocking errors — errors that prevent them from making progress on the rest of the assignment until resolved. They are working outside of scheduled class hours, typically in the evenings or on weekends, and do not have real-time access to teaching staff.

---

### 2. Context

The problem occurs during self-study sessions outside class hours when the student encounters a blocking error in their code that they cannot resolve independently within 30-60 minutes. The assignment deadline is typically within 2-5 days. The student has already attempted to debug the error themselves and has run out of approaches to try.

---

### 3. Primary Pain

The student cannot get a reliable, relevant answer to their specific code problem within a timeframe that allows them to continue working on the assignment that day. The course forum is monitored during business hours only, with typical response times of 4-7 days. Stack Overflow and similar resources produce generic answers that do not apply to the specific course setup or codebase. Classmates are often equally stuck. The student loses 1-3 hours per blocking error and sometimes abandons the session entirely.

---

### 4. Current Workaround

The student posts the question on the course forum and waits. While waiting, they either attempt to work around the blocked section (which sometimes creates downstream problems) or search Stack Overflow and Google for relevant answers (which rarely resolve course-specific issues). In some cases they message classmates directly on WhatsApp, but classmates are often unable to help.

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When I hit a blocking error on an assignment, I want to get a relevant, understandable explanation within the same study session, so I can keep making progress instead of losing the evening to one problem."

---

### 6. Success Signal

The student gets unblocked within the same study session. They do not lose the entire evening to a single error. They submit an assignment they are satisfied with because they were not forced to submit a broken section they could not debug.

---

### 7. Where to Find Them

- First and second-year CS students we know personally through our own programme
- The KIU CS first-year WhatsApp group (we have access through friends)
- Students visible in the CS computer lab during evening hours

---

### 8. Why You, Why Now

Two team members experienced first-year CS. One is currently in second year. We have personal networks in these cohorts and can schedule interviews immediately.

---
