## Component 1: Four Filters Evaluation

**File location:** `00-foundation/four-filters-evaluation.md`

### Strong Submission

- All problem candidates from `all-problem-statements.md` are represented in the evaluation
- Every candidate is scored on all four filters (not just the top candidates)
- Every score is accompanied by at least one sentence of written justification — not a number alone
- The justification is grounded in observable evidence, not speculation ("three team members have personally experienced this weekly" vs "this seems like it would be common")
- The workaround description for Filter 2 is specific and concrete — names the actual tools or behaviours used
- The scorecard summary table is complete
- The shortlist of top 2-3 candidates is stated with reasoning
- The team decision and rationale are recorded
- Team discussion notes reflect genuine deliberation, including any disagreements

### Weak Submission (marks deducted)

- Scores without written justification — numbers without reasoning are not evaluations
- Justifications that are assertions without evidence ("this is a real problem" without naming who has it)
- Candidates evaluated for only some filters
- Workaround listed as "none" or "they just deal with it" without genuine exploration
- Scorecard table incomplete or missing
- Decision section blank or vague ("we chose Problem 1 because it was the best")

### Missing (significant marks deducted)

- File not present
- Fewer than three problem candidates evaluated (suggests Lab 1 deliverables were inadequate)
- No evidence of structured evaluation — appears to be post-hoc rationalisation of a decision already made

## Component 2: Team Problem Statement

**File location:** `00-foundation/team-problem-statement.md`

### Strong Submission

- Uses the formula: specific user segment, specific pain, specific context, specific consequence, specific workaround, specific reason the workaround is inadequate
- The user segment is precise enough to identify a real person within 10 minutes
- The pain is described as an experience, not a cause
- The consequence is something meaningful to the user, not just an annoyance
- The workaround names actual tools or behaviours, not a generic description
- All team members are listed as having agreed to the statement
- The evidence basis section indicates what the statement is based on (experience, observation, research)

### Weak Submission (marks deducted)

- User segment is too broad to be actionable ("university students", "people who use apps")
- Pain is described as a cause rather than a felt experience ("they don't have a tool" vs "they lose 30 minutes every time this happens")
- Consequence is absent or trivial
- Workaround is vague or missing
- Statement reads as a solution pitch disguised as a problem ("people need a better way to...")

### Missing (significant marks deducted)

- File not present
- Statement is a single sentence with no detail
- Statement describes a solution rather than a problem

## Component 3: Ideal Customer Profiles

**File location:** `00-foundation/team-icp.md`

Both a primary and a secondary ICP are required.

### Strong Submission

For each ICP:
- All eight dimensions are completed: segment, context, primary pain, current workaround, job to be done, success signal, where to find them, why you why now
- The segment is specific enough to identify a real person
- The workaround dimension is not blank — if you do not know the workaround yet, note this explicitly and explain how you will find out
- The JTBD is written in the correct format: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]"
- Where to find them names specific places or communities, not just "on campus"
- The evidence basis table is completed with honest confidence ratings
- The secondary ICP is as complete as the primary — not a rushed afterthought

### Weak Submission (marks deducted)

- Secondary ICP is significantly less complete than primary (suggests it was rushed at the last minute)
- Workaround section is blank or says "we don't know yet" without noting this as an open question to investigate
- Segment is too broad to be actionable
- JTBD is not in the correct format or describes a solution rather than a motivation
- "Where to find them" is vague — "on campus" or "in the CS department" without specific locations or communities

### Missing (significant marks deducted)

- File not present
- Only one ICP submitted
- ICPs copied from the example without modification

## Component 4: Interview Script v1

**File location:** `01-discovery/interview-script-v1.md`

A minimum of 10 questions per problem candidate is required. If you have two problem candidates (primary and secondary), you need a script for each — at least 10 questions in each script, or one script of 10+ questions that covers both if they share the same ICP.

### Strong Submission

- Minimum 10 questions present per problem candidate
- Questions span all five phases: opening, problem exploration, problem deep dive, current solutions, close
- All questions are past-tense or present-tense about behaviour — no future-tense hypotheticals
- No leading questions — questions do not suggest the answer
- Questions are open-ended — not answerable with yes or no
- The introduction text is personalised to the problem space (not copied verbatim from the template)
- The screener questions are present and relevant to the ICP
- The post-interview checklist is present
- The notes section shows thought about the specific interview context

### Weak Submission (marks deducted)

- Hypothetical future questions present ("Would you use...", "Would you pay...")
- Leading questions present ("Don't you find it frustrating when...")
- Questions are mostly yes/no answerable
- Fewer than 10 questions per candidate
- Questions are generic and could apply to any problem (no specificity to this ICP and problem)
- All five phases not represented — missing the close or the problem deep dive

### Missing (significant marks deducted)

- File not present in correct location
- Script has fewer than 6 questions
- Script is the template with placeholder text not filled in

## Component 5: Interview Tracker

**File location:** `01-discovery/outreach/interview-tracker.md`

### Strong Submission

- At least 5 contacts identified by Wednesday 25 March
- Each contact entry includes: how they match the ICP, outreach method, date contacted, current status
- Contacts appear to be real people the team can actually reach — not invented or vague ("a friend", "some students")
- At least one contact is listed as "Scheduled" or "Contacted" with a date
- Outreach message templates are present and personalised to the problem space

### Weak Submission (marks deducted)

- Fewer than 5 contacts
- Contacts are vague or clearly invented
- No outreach status logged — contacts listed but no evidence of outreach attempted
- ICP match reason not provided for contacts

### Missing (significant marks deducted)

- File not present
- File is the template with no contacts filled in

## Overall Assessment Levels

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Excellent | All five components strong. Evidence of genuine thinking at each stage. Problem statement and ICP are specific enough to act on immediately. Interview script is ready to use. |
| Good | Most components strong with minor weaknesses. Perhaps one ICP is thin, or the interview script has 1-2 hypothetical questions that should be revised. |
| Satisfactory | Components present but with notable gaps. Problem statement is too broad. One ICP is incomplete. Interview script has multiple leading or hypothetical questions. |
| Needs Improvement | Multiple components weak or missing. Problem statement is vague. ICPs lack specificity. Interview script is not Mom Test-compliant. Resubmission encouraged. |
