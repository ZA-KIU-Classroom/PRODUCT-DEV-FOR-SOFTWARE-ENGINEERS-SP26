## Context

In Lab 1 you formed your team and each member wrote three individual problem statements. You left with no committed problem. That was deliberate.

In Lab 2 you apply the frameworks from Lecture 2 (The Mom Test, Jobs to Be Done, the Four Filters, and the Ideal Customer Profile) to make a real commitment. By the end of this session your team will have selected one problem to pursue and will have the interview preparation materials needed to go and talk to real people this week.

This is the first high-stakes decision your team makes together. Take it seriously.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab you will be able to:

1. Apply the Four Filters framework to evaluate competing problem candidates systematically.
2. Commit to a single problem statement as a team using structured criteria.
3. Write a precise Ideal Customer Profile for your chosen problem.
4. Construct a Mom Test-compliant interview script with at least ten questions.
5. Develop an outreach strategy and begin scheduling interviews with real people.

## Grading

Participation and labwork marks are not given for attendance. They are earned through the quality of submitted work.

| Component | What we are assessing |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Four Filters evaluation | All candidates scored on all four filters with written reasoning, not just numbers |
| Problem statement | Specific user, specific context, specific pain, specific consequence. Not generic. |
| ICPs | Two complete profiles using the full ICP template, grounded in real observations |
| Interview script | 10+ questions per candidate. No leading questions. No hypotheticals. Mom Test-compliant. |
| Interview tracker | At least 5 real contacts identified, outreach method noted, status tracked |

One resubmission is permitted within one week of the deadline for up to 80% of lost marks.
