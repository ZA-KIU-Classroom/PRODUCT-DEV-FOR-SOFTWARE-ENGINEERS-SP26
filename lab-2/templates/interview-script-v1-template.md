## Script Purpose

This script is a guide, not a rigid questionnaire. Use it to structure the conversation, but follow interesting threads when the interviewee takes you somewhere unexpected. The goal is to learn, not to complete a checklist.

Every question in this script must pass the Mom Test:
- Ask about past behaviour, not future intentions
- Ask about the problem, not your solution
- No leading questions
- No hypotheticals

## Interview Introduction (Deliver this, do not read it word-for-word)

"Thanks for taking the time to speak with me. I'm working on a university project where we're trying to understand [general topic area — do not mention your solution]. I'm not pitching anything — I just want to understand how things work for you. There are no right or wrong answers. I might take some notes, but everything you tell me is just to help us understand the problem space better. Does that sound okay?

Is it okay if I [record this / take notes]?"

## Phase 1: Opening and Context (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand this person's world and daily routine in the relevant area. Do not mention your problem yet.

**Question 1:**
[Write an open question about their general routine or context in the relevant area]
Example: "Walk me through what a typical day on campus looks like for you."

**Question 2:**
[Write a follow-up that narrows to the relevant domain]
Example: "What do you do between classes? How do you usually spend that time?"

**Follow-up prompts to have ready:**
- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "How long have you been doing it that way?"
- "Does that work well for you?"

---

## Phase 2: Problem Exploration (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Find out whether the problem shows up in their life without prompting it. Let them bring it up if possible.

**Question 3:**
[Write a broad question about frustrations or friction in this area]
Example: "What are the most frustrating or time-wasting parts of your time on campus?"

**Question 4:**
[Write a question that probes for workarounds or coping behaviour]
Example: "Is there anything in your day where you find yourself doing something annoying just to get by?"

**Question 5:**
[Write a question that probes for a specific recent experience of friction]
Example: "Tell me about a time recently when something about [relevant area] really didn't go the way you wanted."

**If they mention your target problem unprompted:** Note this — it is your strongest signal. Ask them to tell you more. Do not reveal that this is your focus.

**If they do not mention your target problem:** It is okay to gently introduce the domain after Phase 2. "One thing I've been thinking about is [general area, not your solution]. Has that ever come up for you?"

---

## Phase 3: Problem Deep Dive (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand the problem with enough specificity and depth to write a meaningful problem statement and design accurate interview questions for future interviews.

**Question 6:**
[Write a question that asks them to walk through a specific past instance of the problem]
Example: "Tell me about the last time that happened. Walk me through it from beginning to end."

**Question 7:**
[Write a Five Whys-style question to get to root cause]
Example: "When that happens, what do you do? Why that approach?"
[Plan to ask "why?" at least two more times in response to their answer]

**Question 8:**
[Write a question about consequences and impact]
Example: "What happens as a result of that? What does it cost you?"

**Five Whys reminder:** When they explain a behaviour, ask "why?" not to be annoying but to reach the real cause. Surface behaviours are symptoms. You want root causes.

---

## Phase 4: Current Solutions (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand exactly what they are currently using or doing to address this problem, and how satisfied they are.

**Question 9:**
[Write a question about existing tools or solutions they have tried]
Example: "Have you ever tried anything — an app, a system, anything — to deal with this? What happened?"

**Question 10:**
[Write a question about satisfaction with the current workaround]
Example: "How well does [whatever they described] actually work for you? What does it not do?"

**Optional additional question:**
[Write a question about willingness to pay or switch — only if the problem has commercial implications]
Example: "Have you ever paid for anything to help with this kind of thing?"

---

## Phase 5: Close (5 minutes — 1 question minimum)

Goal: Gather any remaining insights, ask for referrals, and leave on good terms.

**Question 11 (catch-all):**
"Is there anything about this area that I haven't asked you about but that you think is important or interesting?"

**Referral question:**
"Is there anyone else you know who deals with this kind of thing regularly? I'd love to talk to them — would you be okay to introduce me?"

**Close:**
"This has been incredibly helpful. I really appreciate your time. If anything comes up that's relevant, would it be okay if I sent you a quick message?"
